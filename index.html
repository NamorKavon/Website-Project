About me

My name is Roman Novak, I have a longstanding background in IT and in every job I’ve held there has been some degree of technology usage. I started out building gaming PCs after dropping out of college on completion of my first year and that kickstarted my passion for the technology industry. Using a PC regularly just a couple of years after I started talking I am very comfortable navigating my way around a computer and understand on an instinctual level how to troubleshoot problems on a multitude of applications and the internet.

I made the decision to join the CodeNation course on the rationale that it would be an entry into a career perhaps in software development or even website design. My experience with CodeNation so far has been an intriguing and intellectually stimulating affair, in which I’ve learnt just how little I know and conversely how much there is to learn moving forward. The general atmosphere of the office is positive and full of character, something I find important in creating a workplace that makes you want to turn up everyday. I’m a huge proponent of active learning and CodeNation provides the ultimate method to learn, a crash course in Python which ends in a test of your knowledge by building a simple text-based adventure game and then moving onto an introduction into HTML which this project is based on. I am not a great fan of spending several years in University learning all of the theory and having no real world experience when I finish so this course is a great way to gain those real world skills but also still learning the key aspects of software development.
